\chapter*{General Introduction}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{General Introduction}

Football clubs increasingly need digital tools to organize their internal operations in a clear and centralized way. In this context, the management of events, documents, player information, notifications, and user permissions becomes an important aspect of the club's daily workflow. When these elements are not grouped within a unified platform, information can become scattered, access control becomes harder to manage, and collaboration between different members of the organization may lose efficiency.

This final year project, entitled \textbf{Football OS}, aims to design and implement a centralized workspace dedicated to football club operations. The platform brings together several essential features, including event management, document management, player profiles, notifications, and workspace search. It also relies on a modular backend architecture that separates responsibilities between services, manages permissions, and supports a more organized and scalable structure for future development.

The main problem addressed by this project can therefore be stated as follows: \textbf{how can we design a centralized, structured, and scalable digital platform that facilitates the internal management of a football club while respecting the roles, permissions, and operational needs of its different users?}

To answer this problem, the report is organized into several chapters. The first chapter presents the general context of the project, its objectives, and the adopted work methodology. The second chapter focuses on the functional analysis of the solution by identifying the actors, requirements, and main use cases. The third chapter describes the technical architecture and the main design choices related to the backend, frontend, and supporting services. The fourth chapter presents the implementation of the project, including the developed features, their integration, and the obtained results. Finally, the general conclusion summarizes the completed work, the difficulties encountered, and the possible future improvements.
